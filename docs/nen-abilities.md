Aura Control Techniques (Core System)

This document describes the fundamental aura control techniques used by all characters.
These techniques represent basic mastery over life energy and are learned before developing any personal signature abilities.

These techniques are universal.
Every trained user can learn them regardless of their affinity.

They form the foundation of combat, perception, defense, and aura management.

Overview

The core techniques are:

Technique	Purpose
Ten	Maintain aura around the body for protection and stability
Zetsu	Completely suppress aura output
Ren	Intensify aura output for power
Gyo	Focus aura into a specific body part
En	Expand aura outward to detect surroundings
In	Hide aura from perception
Shu	Extend aura into objects
Ko	Concentrate all aura into one point
Ken	Sustained defensive aura state
Ryu	Dynamic aura distribution during combat

Not every character must begin with all techniques unlocked.
Progression systems should unlock them gradually.

Ten
Concept

Ten is the technique of maintaining a thin layer of aura around the body at all times.

It prevents aura from leaking away and forms the most basic defensive barrier.

Without Ten, a person's aura dissipates constantly.

Functional Purpose

Ten acts as the baseline aura control state.

It provides:

passive defense

aura stability

aura retention

resistance to hostile aura pressure

Gameplay Behavior

Ten should be the default aura state once the player learns it.

Effects may include:

small passive damage reduction

slow aura regeneration

protection from environmental aura effects

improved resistance to fear or intimidation effects

Example Gameplay Properties

Possible mechanics:

+10–20% damage resistance

+small aura regeneration

aura leakage prevention

immunity to weak aura pressure

Visual Representation

Ten should visually appear as:

a faint aura layer surrounding the body

subtle shimmering or flowing energy

Zetsu
Concept

Zetsu is the technique of completely shutting off aura output.

The user closes their aura nodes and stops aura flow entirely.

Functional Purpose

Zetsu removes all aura presence.

This makes the user:

undetectable to aura sensing

extremely vulnerable

Gameplay Behavior

When using Zetsu:

Advantages:

cannot be detected by aura sensing abilities

stealth advantages

reduced aura consumption

improved stamina recovery

Disadvantages:

no defensive aura

much higher damage taken

cannot use aura techniques

Example Gameplay Properties

Possible mechanics:

Advantages:

invisibility to aura detection

faster stamina recovery

reduced enemy awareness

Disadvantages:

+200% incoming damage

aura abilities disabled

Visual Representation

Zetsu should appear as:

aura disappearing completely

calm, quiet presence

Ren
Concept

Ren is the technique of expanding and strengthening one's aura output.

The user releases a large amount of aura and maintains it under control.

Functional Purpose

Ren dramatically increases aura intensity.

It improves:

offensive strength

intimidation

aura pressure

ability potency

Gameplay Behavior

Ren is an offensive amplification state.

Effects may include:

increased damage

increased aura consumption

stronger ability effects

intimidation effects on weaker enemies

Example Gameplay Properties

Possible mechanics:

+30–50% attack power

faster aura consumption

increased knockback force

aura pressure affecting weak enemies

Visual Representation

Ren should appear as:

intense aura flaring outward

strong visible aura waves

Gyo
Concept

Gyo is the technique of focusing aura into a specific part of the body.

Most commonly it is used in the eyes for enhanced perception.

Functional Purpose

Gyo increases the precision and effectiveness of aura usage.

Common uses:

detecting hidden aura

enhancing strikes

reinforcing a specific limb

Gameplay Behavior

Gyo should be a short-term focus ability.

Possible uses:

detect hidden enemies

reveal concealed aura

temporarily boost attack power of a limb

improve perception of traps or aura constructs

Example Gameplay Properties

Possible mechanics:

reveal hidden objects

detect aura signatures

+precision damage

short duration focus state

Visual Representation

Gyo typically appears as:

aura concentrated into the eyes or limb

glowing eyes during perception

En
Concept

En is the technique of expanding one's aura outward into the surrounding space.

This creates a detection field.

Anything entering that field can be sensed instantly.

Functional Purpose

En functions as a spatial awareness system.

It allows the user to sense:

living beings

moving objects

hostile intent

aura signatures

Gameplay Behavior

En should act as an active perception field.

Effects may include:

detecting enemies through walls

detecting hidden aura users

detecting movement within a radius

early warning system

Example Gameplay Properties

Possible mechanics:

detection radius around player

aura drain proportional to radius

enemy movement alerts

stealth countermeasure

Balance Considerations

En should be expensive to maintain.

The larger the radius:

the higher the aura drain

the harder it is to maintain

In
Concept

In is the technique of concealing one's aura.

Unlike Zetsu, aura still exists but becomes extremely difficult to perceive.

Functional Purpose

In is primarily used for:

stealth

hiding aura constructs

ambush tactics

Gameplay Behavior

Possible effects:

conceal aura from detection abilities

hide aura-based traps

make aura weapons invisible

However, users employing Gyo may still detect hidden aura.

Example Gameplay Properties

Possible mechanics:

stealth aura masking

hidden traps

hidden projectiles

Shu
Concept

Shu is the technique of extending aura into objects.

This reinforces them and allows them to carry aura power.

Functional Purpose

Shu allows weapons or tools to be enhanced.

Examples:

reinforcing a sword

strengthening thrown objects

protecting equipment

Gameplay Behavior

Possible effects:

weapon damage increase

object durability increase

aura-infused projectiles

Ko
Concept

Ko is the technique of focusing all aura into a single point.

Typically used for devastating attacks.

Functional Purpose

Ko sacrifices defense to maximize attack power.

Gameplay Behavior

Possible mechanics:

Advantages:

massive attack power increase

Disadvantages:

no defensive aura elsewhere

extremely vulnerable

Ken
Concept

Ken is the technique of sustaining strong aura output for defense.

It is essentially a long-term defensive state.

Functional Purpose

Ken is used to endure long battles.

It increases durability and stability.

Gameplay Behavior

Possible effects:

high defense

high aura upkeep

resistance to knockback

Ryu
Concept

Ryu is the technique of dynamically distributing aura across the body during combat.

Instead of focusing aura in one location permanently, the user shifts it continuously.

Functional Purpose

Ryu represents advanced combat mastery.

It allows the user to:

defend and attack simultaneously

adapt to enemy actions

redirect aura where needed

Gameplay Behavior

Possible effects:

dynamic damage reduction

dynamic attack boosts

skilled timing advantage

This technique should reward high player skill.

Suggested Learning Order

For gameplay progression, the techniques might unlock in this order:

Ten

Zetsu

Ren

Gyo

Shu

En

In

Ken

Ko

Ryu

This progression allows players to gradually understand aura mechanics.

Summary

Core aura techniques define how characters manipulate aura, not what unique powers they possess.

These techniques provide:

defense

stealth

perception

power amplification

weapon reinforcement

spatial awareness

combat mastery

They form the foundation of all advanced abilities.

Signature abilities are built on top of these mechanics, not in place of them.
